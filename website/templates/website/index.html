Hi my name is Mike and I am a self-taught Python developer specialising in backend development. Since finishing my degree in philosophy I have been teaching myself software development and have recently begun a self-funded development course with Just IT. I decided to invest in myself and take the course in order to provide structure to my study and to give me the confidence that I have a well rounded understanding of the skills necessary to succeed as a developer. Over the course of my philosophy degree I developed strong problem solving skills and the ability to analyse and break apart complex abstract problems building on skills learned over the course of my study of mathematics. In addition to improving my ability to communicate abstract ideas to explain myself to the team I am currently working on a problem with.

Over my development career I have built a website using Django that allows users to guess which team is going to win a sports match; a GUI based application using Tkinter that recommends players for the users fantasy American football team over the course of a draft and a web scraper to collect data for the database used by the application. Mathematics Philosophy Software Development

I think that software development is a natural progression from both mathematics and philosophy because all three disciplines are based on a strong ability to think logically and analyse a problem; break it down into simpler parts and then solve those simple problems in order to find a solution to the original complex problem. Software development combines the symbolic and formal logic systems of mathematics with the broader scope of philosophy and uses abstract problem solving to allow you to build a concrete product that can be of more practical benefit and that is valuable to a much wider audience.
